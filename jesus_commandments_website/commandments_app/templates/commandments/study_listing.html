{% trans 'Here are all the points we took in consideration while studying all the unique steps which Jesus taught' %}:
  • {% trans 'Read everything Jesus said, starting with Matthew, then Mark, Luke and John' %}.
  • {% trans 'Focus on all commandments Jesus gave, and be aware of the following differences:' %}.
      • {% trans 'Is it a "principle" or a "value"?' %}
    • {% trans 'A "principle" is generic which applies to all generations, no matter what culture, time or place' %}.
      {% trans 'By example: "Love your neighbors as yourself" (Matthew 19:19)' %}
    • {% trans 'A "value" is the way how a principle would pack out in a specific culture, time and place. If you have this, you need to derive the principle out of it by studying the context' %}.
      {% trans 'By example: "Salute one another with an holy kiss" (Rom 16:16)' %}. {% trans 'The principle is not kiss everybody, but to salute your brothers and sisters in an intimate way, based on your specific culture, time and place' %}.
      • {% trans 'Is it "literal" or "figurative"?' %}
    • {% trans 'A "literal" commandment is literally what Jesus is saying which you can directly obey and apply' %}.
      {% trans 'By example: "Worship the Lord your God, and serve him only" (Matthew 4:10)' %}
    • {% trans 'A "figurative" commandment is descriptive or a parable (Usually a short fictitious story that illustrates a moral attitude or a religious principle)' %}.
      {% trans 'By example: "The parable of the Good Samaritan " (Luke 10:29-37)' %}. {% trans 'The principle is to be merciful to those who crosses your paths' %}.
      • {% trans 'Is it "specific" or a "generic"?' %}
    • {% trans 'There are also some specific commandments which Jesus gave specifically to that person in that time, which will not directly apply for us today' %}.
      {% trans 'By example: "Go your way into the village over against you: and as soon as ye be entered into it, ye shall find a colt tied, whereon never man sat; loose him, and bring him." (Mark 11:2)' %}
      • {% trans 'Is it a "negative" or "positive" commandment?' %}
    • {% trans 'A negative commandment is something you should not do. To all negative commandments, there is an opposite positive commandment. In the unique primary commandments, we mention only positive commandments, while in the references you will also find lots of the opposite negative commandments. So if you have a prohibition, derive the opposite commandment from it' %}.
      {% trans 'By example: "That whosoever looketh on a woman to lust after her hath committed adultery with her already in his heart (Matthew 5:28). The negative commandment is to not look with lust to another woman, the commandment is to stay loyal to your partner (Honor marriage) and keep your heart pure' %}.
      • {% trans 'Should you "continually", or "start" doing it?' %}
    • {% trans 'There is a difference in "immediately start doing it" or "keep continually or habitually doing it"' %}.
      {% trans 'By example: "Guard against self-deception, each of you. If someone among you thinks he is wise in this age, let him become foolish so that he can become wise (1 Cor 3:18). The progressive type here is to "Guard continually" and to "become immediately foolish" if you think you are wise' %}.

  • {% trans 'If it is new:' %}
      1. {% trans 'Add it with its primary Bible reference' %}
      2. {% trans 'Determine a short powerful title which summarizes the commandment. Try to stick as close as possible to the Words of Jesus Himself (Only summarize when necessary)' %}
      3. {% trans 'Determine in which "category" the commandment falls' %}
  • {% trans 'If it is an already existing, determine to which existing commandment(s) it should apply' %}'
      • {% trans 'Is it a "Primary", "Direct", "Indirect", "Example", "Duplicate", "OT Law", "Wisdom" or "Study" reference?' %}
    • {% trans 'Primary' %}: {% trans 'This Primary Bible reference is a unique Bible reference according to the words of Jesus, which best summarizes the commandment' %}.
    • {% trans 'Direct' %}: {% trans 'Extra direct Bible references which expands or explain the same commandment' %}.
    • {% trans 'Indirect' %}: {% trans 'Extra, more indirect Bible references, relating to the same commandment' %}.
    • {% trans 'Example' %}: {% trans 'In these Bible references you will find examples and testimonies how this commandment is being executed' %}.
    • {% trans 'Duplicate' %}: {% trans 'Another Bible reference to more or less the same as the primary, direct or indirect, with not much new information, although it will confirm the same Bible references' %}:
    • {% trans 'OT Law' %}: {% trans 'Old Testament Law Bible references which will relate to OT Commandments (Jewish tradition teached that there are 613 commandments or mitzvot in the Torah)' %}:
